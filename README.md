# Architecture Computational Tools Library

This repository was created as part of the research activities of the Department of Architecture, University of Thessaly. Its purpose is to collect, share, and expand any kind of material that supports the design and research workflow of architecture students — no matter how finished or unfinished it may be.

## Purpose

The tools and resources gathered here aim to improve the efficiency of architectural workflows and preserve knowledge that would otherwise be lost after a project ends. The repository is addressed to architecture students at any level, whether they are just starting out with digital tools or looking to share something they have been developing for a while.

This is not just a library of finished tools. It is also a place for ideas that never had the chance to be completed, workflows discovered along the way, and notes or sketches that might be useful to someone else. Over time, the repository will take its own shape based on what the community brings to it.

## Contents

The repository currently includes the following folders:

- **Grasshopper** — parametric design scripts, workflows, and automation tools
- **AutoCAD** — blocks, tools, templates, and workflows
- **Rhino** — creative patterns, constructions, and unfinished ideas worth sharing
- **Other** — Photoshop templates, notes, sketches, references, and anything else related to the school and the design process that does not fit elsewhere

New categories will be added as the library grows.

## How to Use

1. Browse the folders and find the material relevant to your project
2. Download the `.gh`, `.pdf`, `.dwg`, `.3dm` or any other file
3. Each folder includes descriptions or instructions where available
4. Adapt everything freely to your own work

## How to Contribute

If you want to add something — finished or not:

1. **Fork** this repository
2. Add your file inside the relevant folder along with a short description
3. Submit a **Pull Request** with a brief note on what it is and what problem it solves
4. The maintainer will review and merge it

You can also send your files directly to uthopen@gmail.com and we will add them for you.

All contributions are credited to their authors.
